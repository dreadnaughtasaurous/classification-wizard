## In short

This wizard checks whether a named-position override applies, then — if not — scores a role against six factors from Schedule 3D Part 1, combines those six scores into one weighted total, and maps that total to a grade. Two safety nets sit on top: a check for scores that land close to a grade boundary, and a check for answer patterns that look inconsistent (e.g. high leadership but low accountability). Either safety net can trigger a recommendation to have HR review the result before it's finalised.

This tool does **not** make a final classification decision. It provides a recommendation only. HR must confirm the final classification.

---

## Relationship to the Agreement

The wizard is based on **Schedule 3D, Part 1** of the *Health and Allied Services, Managers and Administrative Workers (Victorian Public Sector) (Single Interest Employers) Enterprise Agreement 2025–2027*.

The grade descriptors in the Agreement remain the source of truth. The scoring model below is a structured decision-support method for applying those descriptors consistently — it does not override them.

The wizard does not replace:

* HR review;
* formal position description assessment;
* consultation with relevant stakeholders;
* consideration of organisational structure;
* the Agreement itself; or
* any applicable policy or delegation framework.

---

## What the wizard assesses

The wizard assesses the role against six domains. These reflect the main themes that recur across the Schedule 3D grade descriptors, from Grade 1A through to Grade 11: lower grades emphasise routine work, defined procedures and close monitoring, while higher grades emphasise delegated accountability, strategic judgement, major service impact and whole-of-health-service responsibility.

1. **Knowledge, Skills & Experience (KSE)** — the experience, technical knowledge, qualifications or specialist expertise normally required for the role.
2. **Problem-Solving & Judgement (PSJ)** — the complexity of problems the role deals with, and whether it follows established procedures or develops new approaches.
3. **Accountability & Autonomy (AA)** — how closely the role is monitored, what decisions it can make, and what it is accountable for.
4. **Leadership & Team Management (LTM)** — the role's responsibility for supervising, coordinating, managing or directing staff, teams, functions or services.
5. **Communication & Interpersonal Influence (CII)** — the level of persuasion, liaison, negotiation or stakeholder influence required.
6. **Organisational Impact & Scope (OIS)** — the size and significance of the role's impact, from a single work area through to whole-service or system-level responsibility.

A role rarely sits at the same level on all six. A technical specialist might score high on KSE but lower on LTM; an executive role should generally score high on AA, CII and OIS together. The radar chart on the results screen (see below) is designed to show that pattern.

## Before scoring starts

**Named-position override check.** The Agreement names a small number of positions and mandates their grade directly, without reference to the scoring model. If the role matches one of these, the wizard applies that grade immediately and skips all remaining questions:

| Named position | Mandated grade | Clause |
|---|---:|---|
| Interpreter, unqualified | Grade 1 | 3.4(a) |
| NAATI-accredited interpreter/translator — one other language | Grade 2 | 4.4(a) |
| Workplace Trainer / Careers Advisor | Grade 3 | 5.4(a) |
| NAATI-accredited interpreter/translator — two or more languages | Grade 3 | 5.4(b) |
| Manager of Dental Maintenance Technicians (work allocation, training, rostering, guidance) | Grade 3 | 5.4(c) |

If no override applies, the wizard moves on to scoring.

## How questions are scored

Each of the 24 questions (four per domain) offers five options, each worth a fixed point value:

| Option | Points | General meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| A | 0 | Very limited, routine or closely defined work |
| B | 25 | Developing or routine work with some experience, training or judgement |
| C | 50 | Senior operational, technical, supervisory or function-level work |
| D | 75 | Senior management, specialist, major-function or agency-wide work |
| E | 100 | Executive, strategic, whole-service or system-level work |

These points describe the *maturity level* of the role against that specific question — not a percentage of the role's duties.

**Domain score** = average of the four question scores in that domain. This gives six domain scores, each 0–100, shown on the radar chart on the results screen.

## Why the domains are weighted differently

The Agreement doesn't prescribe a scoring formula or fixed percentage weights. The wizard uses a structured job-evaluation model, reasoned from the wording progression across the Schedule 3D grade descriptors — grounded in standard job-evaluation practice, but **not itself a value stated in the Agreement**.

The reasoning: different factors matter more at different points in the grade structure.

| Weighting band | What matters more | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Grades 1A–3** | KSE, PSJ, AA | These grades are distinguished mainly by experience, training, procedural knowledge and the degree of supervision. |
| **Grades 4–7** | AA, LTM | These grades are distinguished by managing work programs, controlling resources, supervising staff and being accountable for functions or budgets. |
| **Grades 8–11** | OIS, CII, AA | These grades are distinguished by whole-service impact, strategic accountability, executive delegation and major stakeholder negotiation. |

The exact per-domain weights for each band:

| Domain | Grades 1A–3 | Grades 4–7 | Grades 8–11 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Knowledge, Skills & Experience | 25% | 18% | 12% |
| Problem-Solving & Judgement | 25% | 18% | 16% |
| Accountability & Autonomy | 20% | 22% | 18% |
| Leadership & Team Management | 10% | 22% | 16% |
| Communication & Interpersonal Influence | 10% | 10% | 18% |
| Organisational Impact & Scope | 10% | 10% | 20% |
| **Total** | **100%** | **100%** | **100%** |

**Which band applies is decided automatically**, from a preliminary, *unweighted* average of the six domain scores:

| Unweighted average | Band used |
|---:|---|
| 0.00–33.99 | Grades 1A–3 |
| 34.00–69.99 | Grades 4–7 |
| 70.00–100.00 | Grades 8–11 |

This preliminary average is only used to pick the band — it is not the final score.

## Worked example

Say a manager is scoring a **Team Leader** role and answers land as follows:

| Domain | Domain score |
|---|---:|
| KSE | 50 |
| PSJ | 50 |
| AA | 62.5 |
| LTM | 62.5 |
| CII | 37.5 |
| OIS | 37.5 |

**Unweighted average** = (50+50+62.5+62.5+37.5+37.5) ÷ 6 = **50.0** → falls in the 34.00–69.99 range, so the **Grades 4–7 band** applies (AA and LTM weighted heaviest).

**Weighted total** = (50×18%) + (50×18%) + (62.5×22%) + (62.5×22%) + (37.5×10%) + (37.5×10%)
= 9 + 9 + 13.75 + 13.75 + 3.75 + 3.75 = **53.0**

**Grade lookup**: 53.0 falls in the 52.00–60.99 range → **recommended grade: Grade 6**.

The wizard would then run the boundary and mismatch checks described below against this same result.

## Two safety nets: when the wizard suggests HR review

A review recommendation does **not** mean the grade is wrong. It means the answer pattern is worth a second look before the classification is finalised. There are two independent checks.

### 1. Boundary proximity

Grade bands aren't all the same width, so "close to a boundary" is measured relative to the width of the two grades either side of it, not as a flat number of points.

* If the score is *reasonably* close to a boundary, the results screen shows the neighbouring grade for reference — this alone doesn't demand HR sign-off.
* If the score is *very* close — close enough that it's a genuine toss-up between two grades — the wizard escalates this to a full "HR review recommended" flag.

In other words: seeing an adjacent grade mentioned on your results isn't automatically a red flag. The red banner only appears when the score is tight enough that small differences in evidence could plausibly change the grade.

### 2. Mismatch checks

The wizard also checks for score combinations that suggest the domains aren't telling a consistent story:

| Pattern | What it may mean |
|---|---|
| High Leadership score, low Accountability & Autonomy score | The role looks like it involves supervision, but the answers don't show formal, delegated decision-making authority. Check whether the leadership role is formal and ongoing. |
| High Knowledge/Skills score, low Organisational Impact score | The role looks highly specialised, but its impact may be confined to a narrow area. Check whether this is a specialist role rather than a broader management role. |
| High Communication/Influence score, low Accountability & Autonomy score | The role looks like it involves significant stakeholder influence, but without matching decision-making authority. Check whether the influence is advisory rather than accountable. |

Any mismatch trigger also produces an "HR review recommended" flag, shown in plain language on the results screen (never as a raw variable name).

## Reading the radar chart

The radar chart shows the six domain scores on a 0–100 scale. A balanced, roughly circular shape means the role scored similarly across domains. An uneven shape is common and not inherently a problem — it usually reflects the genuine shape of the role (e.g. a specialist with high KSE but modest OIS). The chart is there to help you see *why* the score landed where it did, not to replace the calculation itself.

---

## Why the result may not match a job title

The wizard classifies duties, accountabilities and scope — not job titles. "Manager" can mean a small operational team lead, a function head across the service, or an executive-level role, depending on the substance behind the title. Two roles with the same title can land at different grades, and that's expected.
